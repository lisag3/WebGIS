Heat Vulnerability of NYC Neighborhoods

Main Map

What is heat vulnerability?
Exteme heat events are becoming more common due to global climate change. But not everyone is affected equally by heatwaves. Black and low income residents and those with certain health conditions are more likley to be harmed. This map helps visualize areas where residents may be most at risk from exteme heat events. City, state, and federal agencies can use this information to plan mitigation strategies such as planting more urban trees and adding cooling water features to public spaces.

How to use this map.
Use the layer control box in the upper right to choose a data layer to view. Check and uncheck the boxes for Parks and New Cooling Features to toggle their visbility. Hover over a neighborhood to see info on it in the box at top right. Use the search box to search for neighborhoods by name.

Vegetative cover is the percent of land area covered by plants. Vegetation lowers temperatures and reduces heat risks.
The Heat Vulnerability Index (HVI) is a composite score from 1 to 5 showing how vulnerable residents of a neighborhood are to heat emergencies. It uses data on temperature, green space, AC access, poverty, and Black population.
Summer surface temperature is a measure of ground temperature at midday on a hot day. The data was collected by satellite on July 17, 2018.
Parks shows all the NYC parks greater than one acre.
New Cooling Features shows locations of new drinking fountains and spray showers installed in parks through the 2020 Cool It! NYC initiative. Areas with a HVI score of 5 were given priority for new cooling features.
